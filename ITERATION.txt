1) Some of the biggest feedback we got during playtesting was that we lacked novelty, which we addressed by adding
a new portion of gameplay (the driving), updating our guard implementation (vision cone cut off, levels of alertness,
lethality, and more), and honing in on our theming among other features. This makes it so that our gameplay is more
unique within our selected genre, and allows players to engage with it in new ways.

2) A very concrete piece of feedback we received was that our doors should open both ways since our previous implementation
impeded player movement significantly. We now have doors that open away from the player regardless of direction, and they
have a slightly larger opening radius to allow for easier movement.

3) During playtesting, we were told that our controls window was unnecessary due to most controls being intuitive. We fixed
this by removing the panel entirely and adding in-game text with instructions that appear when the player approaches 
interactable objects. This makes it so that players receive the information they need when they need it, as opposed to
dumping a lot on them at the start of the game. We then decided to add the controls information back to the game
as an option in our pause menu, allowing players who have maybe taken a break from the game remind themselves what
controls they need in order to play.

4) A lot of players during our playtesting did not understand that they had limited ammo, and if they did they often found
themselves running out of ammo without realizing it. To remedy this, we moved our ammo HUD to the bottom-center instead of
off to the side, then changed the text to sprites so that the player gets a visual portrayal of their resources.

5) Another piece of big-picture feedback we received was that players wanted more options in regards to how they could
move around the map. We fixed this by creating a more complicated map and adding a lights system that disables a laser
"protecting" the gold. On top of that, we added a vent system that allows players to move around the map without being
detected, but without disabling the lights, there's nothing the player can do about the laser. This makes it so that players
need to choose between extending their journey to turn off the lights and brute forcing their way through the guards.

6) We also were told our gold_spike lacked juice (rightfully so), so we made a few changes to increase the output per
player input. The most prominent visual change was the addition of sprites, and even though there's no animation
yet, they inform the player of their goal (robbing the bank and evading the cops) while looking good. The light system
also changes the aesthetics by dimming the lights and making the laser flicker out. One final addition worth noting
is that when players trip the laser, the entire screen flashes red and blue, indicating that the guards are alerted
to the player's positions.
